MBBS — Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery

The cornerstone of medical education at MNR. Our MBBS program is a 5.5-year comprehensive course covering pre-clinical, para-clinical, and clinical subjects with deep integration of the attached MNR Teaching Hospital.

Students benefit from simulation-based clinical skills labs, anatomy cadaveric dissection halls, digital pathology, and supervised ward postings from the first year.

Duration
5.5 Years (4.5 + 1 Internship)
Seats

MBBS Curriculum

Phase-wise Curriculum Structure

Our MBBS curriculum follows NMC guidelines with Competency-Based Medical Education (CBME) framework.

1

Phase I — Pre-Clinical

Year 1 & Partial Year 2 · Foundation Sciences
  • Anatomy (Gross, Histology, Embryology)
  • Physiology
  • Biochemistry
  • Community Medicine (Introduction)
  • English & Communication Skills
  • Early Clinical Exposure
2

Phase II — Para-Clinical

Year 2 & Year 3 · Integrated Sciences
  • Pathology
  • Microbiology
  • Pharmacology
  • Forensic Medicine & Toxicology
  • Community Medicine
  • Clinical Postings (Concurrent)
3

Phase III — Clinical

Year 3, 4 & 4.5 · Hospital-Based Training
  • General Medicine
  • General Surgery
  • Obstetrics & Gynaecology
  • Paediatrics
  • ENT · Ophthalmology · Orthopaedics
  • Psychiatry · Dermatology · Radiology
4

Compulsory Rotating Internship

1 Year · Supervised Clinical Practice
  • Medicine & Allied Specialities (2 months)
  • Surgery & Allied Specialities (2 months)
  • OBG (2 months)
  • Community Medicine (2 months)
  • Elective Rotation (2 months)
  • Emergency Medicine & ICU (1 month)
