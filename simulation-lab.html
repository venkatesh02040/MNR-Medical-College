World-Class Simulation Lab & Smart Classrooms Ready for Students

By Dr. Meera Nair
Feb 20, 2026
3 min read

MNR Medical College's Clinical Simulation Centre and Smart Classroom Complex are fully commissioned and ready for students — representing one of the most advanced academic infrastructure setups for a newly established medical college in Karnataka.

When MNR Medical College's first students arrive in June 2026, they will step into an academic environment that most medical institutions in India have spent decades working toward. The Clinical Simulation Centre and Smart Classroom Complex — both purpose-built and fully equipped — are now ready, months ahead of the academic session's commencement.

The Clinical Simulation Centre

MNR's simulation centre spans 8,000 square feet across two floors of Block B, housing one of the most comprehensive assemblies of medical training equipment in the region. The centre is designed to allow students to practice clinical skills safely and repeatedly before working with live patients — a model proven to significantly improve clinical outcomes and reduce medical errors.

What's Inside the Simulation Centre

  • High-Fidelity Patient Simulators: Adult, paediatric, and neonatal mannequins that breathe, blink, and respond to treatment — used for emergency medicine, anaesthesia, and resuscitation training
  • Laparoscopy Trainers: Dedicated box trainers and virtual reality laparoscopy simulators for surgical skills development
  • OSCE Stations: 16 standardised Objective Structured Clinical Examination stations for formative and summative clinical assessment
  • Procedural Skills Lab: IV cannulation, suturing, catheterisation, lumbar puncture, and airway management trainers
  • Virtual Dissection Tables: Anatomage virtual dissection tables for anatomy study without cadaveric specimens
  • Debriefing Rooms: Video-enabled debriefing rooms where instructors review simulation sessions with students in real time

Smart Classrooms

All 14 lecture halls and seminar rooms at MNR have been fitted with fully integrated smart classroom technology. Each room features:

  • 4K interactive projectors with touch-enabled annotation boards
  • Live streaming capability for remote learning and recorded lectures
  • Real-time audience response systems for interactive teaching
  • 360-degree lecture capture cameras for AI-enhanced content archiving
  • Tiered seating with individual power and data access points
"The best medical education is not about the number of textbooks on a shelf. It is about placing students in environments where they must think, act, and reflect — safely, repeatedly, and with expert feedback. That is what our simulation centre delivers." — Dr. Ananya Krishnamurthy, Medical Director, MNR Medical College

Simulation Centre at a Glance

8,000 sq ft across 2 floors  ·  High-fidelity adult, paediatric & neonatal simulators  ·  Laparoscopy & VR trainers  ·  16 OSCE stations  ·  Virtual dissection tables  ·  Video-enabled debriefing rooms  ·  14 smart lecture halls

Integration with Hospital Training

MNR's simulation programme is designed to complement — not replace — real clinical training at the attached teaching hospital. Students begin with simulation-based skills in the first two years, then transition to supervised real patient care with the confidence of having already practised those skills hundreds of times in the safety of the simulation centre.

Open for Faculty Demonstration

The simulation centre will host its first faculty demonstration sessions during orientation week in April 2026. Faculty from across all departments will be introduced to the simulation equipment and trained in simulation-based teaching methodologies by MNR's Medical Education Unit. Student orientation to the simulation centre is scheduled for the first week of the academic session in June 2026.
